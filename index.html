BLOG INTERACTIVO: Inglés para personas con discapacidad auditiva

El propósito fundamental de este blog interactivo, es generar un espacio de inclusión en cual tanto personas oyentes como no oyentes tengan a su alcance material didáctico para el aprendizaje del inglés. Así mismo, el presente espacio pretende crear una interacción genuina entre el aprendiz y el material,  de manera tal que pueda adaptarlo a sus condiciones particulares, de manera autónoma o por instrucción de su docente. Es importante recalcar que la transcripción fonética presente en esta página no se basa en el IPA (International Phonetic Alphabet); si bien, sirve como guía, la transcripción usada en el página es un equivalente fonético a la pronunciación, adquisición, y reconocimiento fonético de ciertos alumnos con discapacidad auditiva en el contexto Colombiano.

"Once I cut my toe"

It was back in 1999, a very sunny and beautiful day in Bucaramanga, Colombia, a young 5-year old boy whose main objective in life was playing soccer and riding bicycle. That day, I was playing soccer while my brother was riding his bicycle,so he invited me over:" Fabián, come here with me", I said yes. In that moment I thought that would be a cool idea, but suddenly it turned into the worst idea ever. When I put my foot on the wheel (barefoot to make it worse), he did not give a second to think but started to ride the bike instead. After that, all I remember is me calling  my mother´s name with the  most deafening voice ever, so she  went out right away and asked."What happened"; in shock, my brother just answered: "Nothing mom, we were just playing and riding the bike when the accident happened",.."What acciden" - she asked. My terrified brother answered, " Fabian cut his toe while riding the bicycle with me". Next, my mom called a taxi and ten minutes later I was at the hospital waiting to be assisted. The beautiful nurse washed my foot and took care of the situation.. I was shaking and crying, while my brother was talking to my mom trying to find a valid excuse for the situation.

PAST SIMPLE VS PROGRESSIVE

Past simple is mainly used to describe habits, routines and actions that were completed at a specific time in the past. We tend to use frequency adverbs, or specific times in the past.

* I had a quiz last night

* I always went to school at 6am

Past continuous is mainly used to describe an action happening at the moment, usually interrupted by another action.

* I was eating pizza when my mom called me.

* I was travelling to Barranquilla when my boyfriend had an accident.
